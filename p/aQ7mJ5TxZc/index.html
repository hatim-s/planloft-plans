AI App Builder — N21 Landing Redesign Plan

Locked marketing narrative, demonstration strategy, and performance boundaries.

Decisions locked 2026-07-24.

Decisions

  • Direction: full GSAP cinematic scrollytelling, with ScrollTrigger pinning and scrubbing across narrative sections; Motion is reserved for stray micro-reveals.
  • Hero demo: scripted replay on the inline real renderer using a canned spec and op-list timer—not a live agent or backend. Mobile/reduced-motion gets poster plus WebM fallback.
  • Positioning: fear-primary headline: “Revert the agent's bad run. Keep your own edits.” The stronger “can't destroy your app” promise waits for server-side op-log persistence. Show speed rather than claim it.
  • Sections: pill nav; cinematic hero/replay; build → bad-op villain → revert loop; JSON-spec moat; guarantees; early-access pricing; final CTA; footer.
  • Identity: an app/(marketing)/ route group with a display face and one semantic accent for agent elements, over the studio's shared grayscale base.
  • Component sources: Tailark dusk hero, cult-ui texture overlay, and MagicUI flickering grid—each must be vetted for bundle size and SSR, then adapted to product tokens to avoid a stock look.
  • Delete: remove the ProductMock div rather than restyling it.

Work items

  1. M — Replay harness: frozen demo-spec fixture and op-list player over the runtime renderer, CI smoke test, and lazy mount below the poster.
  2. M — GSAP architecture: ScrollTrigger scaffolding, pinned scrub sections, mobile/reduced-motion degradation, and leaf-component isolation with cleanup.
  3. S/M — Section build: curated component adaptations on marketing tokens.
  4. S — Pricing: Free and roughly $20/month Pro tiers, visibly marked “Early access — billing coming soon,” plus email capture. Capture endpoint/provider is a build-time decision.
  5. S — Copy pass: fear axis, technical moat, and receipts without naming competitors.
  6. S — Performance gate: LCP budget through lazy replay mount, split GSAP bundle, and Lighthouse CI check.
