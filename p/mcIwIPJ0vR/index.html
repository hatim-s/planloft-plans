I.

Philosophy & brand intent

The "why" behind the new look — this shapes every decision the assistant makes.

The origin story helps the assistant make new decisions in the same spirit, not just copy existing screens.

Our inferenceWarm, calm, luxurious, editorial, premium, unhurried — closer to a well-set book or a boutique hotel than to a dashboard.

e.g. calm over density, clarity over decoration, restraint over expressiveness…

II.

Color usage rules

When each family earns its place on screen — and what is off-limits.

Our inferenceThe near-black forest green is rationed: one solid primary button per screen, the count badge next to page titles, checked/selected states of controls (checkboxes, toggles), and small brand accents. It is never used as a background wash, a large surface, or an outlined button.
Our inferenceSand is the "outside" — the page plane, the nav strip, subtle hover on white cards. Once you're inside a white card, fills go warm-gray so content stays quiet; sand doesn't get reused as component fill.
Our inferenceLifecycle status (Live, Draft, Paused, Running) renders as a small colored glyph + regular text — not a pill. Soft tinted pills are reserved for tags, types and categories. Solid-filled status pills are rare, kept for urgent chips like "Undeployed changes".

e.g. pure black, saturated blues, the old purple, neon anything, green-on-sand text, gradients…

III.

Typography

The serif/sans split is the signature of this system — help us state its law precisely.

Our inferenceSerif is reserved for identity: page titles, section titles, and the names of things the user created (automations, apps, objects, users). Everything functional — buttons, labels, table headers, menus, helper text — stays in the sans (Geist). Serif never appears on a button or a form label.
Our inferenceThe system whispers: medium is the loudest weight in everyday chrome; hierarchy is carried by size, serif/sans contrast and color instead of boldness. Semibold/bold are practically absent.
IV.

Layouts & page anatomy

The recurring page skeletons — list pages, detail pages, builders, settings, overviews.

Our inferenceTop to bottom: breadcrumb/workspace bar → serif page title with a dark-green count badge, centered segmented tabs, and right-aligned actions (outlined secondaries, then one solid green primary) → a toolbar row (view selector, refresh, filter, sort on the left; rounded search on the right) → a white, hairline-bordered table card sitting on the sand page.
V.

Components

Buttons, pills, tables, focus, empty states — the everyday vocabulary.

Our inferenceOne solid deep-green primary per screen, always the right-most action. Secondary actions are white with a hairline outline. Tertiary actions are quiet icon buttons or ghost/text buttons. A green outlined button is deliberately not allowed. Destructive stays a muted brick red.
